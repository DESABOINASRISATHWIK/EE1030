\textbf{Question}:\\
A circle has its center at $\myvec{4\\4}$. If one end of a is $\myvec{4\\0}$, then find the coordinates of the other end.
\\
\textbf{Solution: }
\begin{table}[h!]    
    \centering
    \input{tables/table.tex}
    \caption{Input parameters }
    \label{table}
  \end{table}
  Since the centre $\vec O$ is the mid point of $\vec A$ and $\vec B$.
Let $\myvec{x_1\\y_1}$ be the coordinates of $\vec B$,

\begin{align}
	\vec O = \myvec{\frac{x_1+4}{2}\\\frac{y_1+0}{2}}
\end{align}

\begin{align}
	\myvec{\frac{x_1 + 4}{2}\\\frac{y_1 + 0}{2}} = \myvec{4\\4}
\end{align}

This gives us two equations:

\begin{align}
\frac{x_1 + 4}{2} = 4
\end{align}

\begin{align}
\frac{y_1 + 0}{2} = 4
\end{align}

Solving these equations:

1. For \(x_1\):

\begin{align}
\frac{x_1 + 4}{2} = 4
\end{align}

Multiply both sides by 2:

\begin{align}
x_1 + 4 = 8
\end{align}

Subtract 4 from both sides:

\begin{align}
x_1 = 4
\end{align}

2. For \(y_1\):

\begin{align}
\frac{y_1 + 0}{2} = 4
\end{align}

Multiply both sides by 2:

\begin{align}
y_1 = 8
\end{align}

Thus, the coordinates of the other end of the diameter are $\vec B\myvec{4\\8}$.
