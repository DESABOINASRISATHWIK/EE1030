\textbf{Question}:\\
A circle has its center at $\myvec{4\\4}$. If one end of a is $\myvec{4\\0}$, then find the coordinates of the other end.
\\
\textbf{Solution: }
To find the other end of the diameter, we use the fact that the center of the circle is the midpoint of the diameter.

Let \((x_1, y_1)\) be the coordinates of the other end of the diameter. Given that the center of the circle is \((4, 4)\) and one end of the diameter is \((4, 0)\), we have:

\[
	Midpoint = \myvec{\frac{x_1+4}{2}\\\frac{y_1+0}{2}}
\]

Since the midpoint is also the center of the circle, we can set this equal to \((4, 4)\):

\[
	\myvec{\frac{x_1 + 4}{2}\\\frac{y_1 + 0}{2}} = \myvec{4\\4}
\]

This gives us two equations:

\[
\frac{x_1 + 4}{2} = 4
\]

\[
\frac{y_1 + 0}{2} = 4
\]

Solving these equations:

1. For \(x_1\):

\[
\frac{x_1 + 4}{2} = 4
\]

Multiply both sides by 2:

\[
x_1 + 4 = 8
\]

Subtract 4 from both sides:

\[
x_1 = 4
\]

2. For \(y_1\):

\[
\frac{y_1 + 0}{2} = 4
\]

Multiply both sides by 2:

\[
y_1 = 8
\]

Thus, the coordinates of the other end of the diameter are \myvec{4\\8}.
